Twitter Medication

Our current president tweets like a madman. He mocks people, insults our allies, spreads conspiracy theories, and often just blasts out nonsensical rants. I built this contraption as a personal project to bring to life the feeling of living with this madness. It spits out one painkiller every time the president tweets. The more he tweets, the more painkillers end up in the bowl.

How it works

A script running on a laptop constantly scans twitter to see if the president has posted a new tweet. As soon as a new tweet appears, a command is sent to the microcontroller and a small motor turns 360 degrees, dispensing a pill which falls out of the mouth and into the bowl.

Technical details and gear

I used the Twit library to access the Twitter API, and the Javascript is running via Node.js. The Arduino is hooked up to a driver and a stepper motor, which turns a plastic disc inside a DIY pill dispenser. The pill dispenser was made from some old protein powder tubs, plastic sheets, a funnel, and a bunch of duct tape. The rubber mask is from Amazon.

Code and Reference

These labs on serial communication and serial input from Tom Igoe at NYU ITP were very helpful. After a much simpler earlier design proved unreliable, I based the pill dispensing mechanism on this prototype posted by Frank Charbonier.
